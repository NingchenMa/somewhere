Principles

One thing at a time

Each screen holds a single idea — your words, or a single passage in reply. No cards stacked on cards, no sidebars, no counters demanding attention.

Type is the interface

One neutral sans, two weights, a tight scale. The passage is the largest thing on screen; everything else recedes to small, quiet labels.

Near-monochrome

Off-white paper, near-black ink, three greys for hierarchy. No accent color — restraint is the brand. Hairline rules instead of boxes and shadows.

Quiet motion

A single pulsing dot while it searches; a blinking caret while you write. Nothing spins or slides more than it must.

Space does the work

Generous margins and a tall first gap let the writing breathe. Empty space is treated as a feature, not something to fill.

Under the hood

React + Tailwind. Passages from the free Gutendex API over Project Gutenberg, matched by an agent. Kept passages saved locally in the browser.
